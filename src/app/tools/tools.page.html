Tools for Transforming Negativity:

Imagery Breath Movement

Imagine a composting bin in the center of your belly. The negativity goes into the deep dark earth and gets turned and turned and transformed into fertile rich soil that allows for growth. This composting bin can always be available to you so that you can compost any fears, doubts and anger that arise as you focus on goodness. In this way, the negativity never gets in the way but adds to the fertile ground.

Imagine a fire in your gut burning up the negativity that arises, crackling and transforming all that you don’t need, giving you heat and power for your breath of goodness.

See the negativity as a color and allow other hues of color to blend with it, elevating into more and more positive vibrancy.

Visualize being cleansed by a rainstorm that bathes your face and body with raindrops of pure water and negative ions that restore you into the experience of your inherent goodness.

Visualize yourself stepping into a shower of light and the light is powerful enough to reconfigure what is arising within you and restore you.

Emotional Balancing Breath

Three short inhale breaths through the nostrils, hold and fill up and full long exhale through the mouth. Continual circular breath, being open to experiencing it cleansing and balancing your emotional body.

Alternate Nostril Breathing

Alternate nostril breathing or “nadi shodhana” — Using the right thumb, softly close the right nostril, and inhale as slowly as you can through the left nostril, then close it with your ring finger. Pause. Open and exhale slowly through the right nostril.

Breath Of Fire

Breath Of Fire is a form of a cleansing pranayama (breathing technique) rooted in Kundalini yoga. Breathe in and out through the nose and start to pull your abdomen in during the exhale, and press it out during the inhale. Your belly fills up with air during the inhale and use your abdominal muscles to push the air out during the exhale, imagining your belly button going in to your spine. Start to shorten each breath and pick up the pace. The breathing should be loud and quick. Equalize the inhale and exhale in both strength and length. This breath is for purification and rejuvenation.

Shake

Standing, your feet hip length apart, rooted into the earth, begin to shake off the negativity and make sounds. Just let your whole body hang towards the earth, chin down to chest, arms down, and then start shaking and sounding out.

Cleanse and Lift into Vertical Alignment

Standing, arms over your head, and begin to circle your arms over your head, in big full circles, using breath and sound to clear out your energy space. Then lift your self into Vertical Alignment, with arms directly over head, feeling feet into ground, see yourself receiving light from above, going through and down your whole body, into the earth.

Chop the Karmic Logs

Stand with your feet further apart than hip length, steady into the earth, parallel to one another, toes straight forward. Swing your arms over head, clasp your hands, imagine holding a axe and take a deep inhale. With the exhale, allow your axe to come down between your legs as if you are chopping logs. Allow a sound of HaH to vibrate out from your belly. Do this again and again until you feel cleansed. You can always shake once you finish before you practice the goodness breath.

Tidbits to Remember:

* When you practice the goodness breath, you may or may not feel goodness. You can’t expect yourself or contract with yourself to feel a specific feeling. It doesn’t work that way. It is the intention and focus that matters.

* You can use your thoughts to direct the practice. With the inhale, your mind can say to yourself — “My essence is goodness” and then with the pause, “I am this goodness. I savor the goodness” and with the exhale, “I offer this goodness out into the world for healing and awakening.”

* You can use your physical body for the practice and create movements to enhance your experience of the goodness breath.

* With your exhale, you can first direct the goodness back into yourself, permeating your whole body with goodness, which then exudes out into the world.

You are encouraged to share your tools and tidbits that you discover works for you by sharing them on our Facebook page.

Ideas for the Practice of The Goodness Breath:

  • Practice it together in circles and gatherings of people.
  • Practice it as you look in the mirror.
  • Practice it in nature and breathe in the goodness of the trees and breathe out the goodness into all of nature.
  • Visualize the Grid of the Earth being lit up everywhere by humans steadfast in knowing goodness.
  • Practice it in dyads with people — Breathing in the other person’s goodness and breathing out your goodness to him or her. Breathing in your goodness and exhaling your goodness to this person before you.
  • Advance practice — Practice it as you listen to the news. This is a very powerful thing to do when you practice this with a group of people.

Practicing the Goodness Breath:

  • Practice it all day, every day, as much as you can remember.
  • Share it with others and practice it in the presence of others.
  • See yourself as a powerful warrior with the goodness breath — there is something you can do, every day. Fiercely practice goodness.
  • Write about it. Talk about it. Practice it with others. Make it yours — and everyone’s!
